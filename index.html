I am a postdoctoral associate at Yale Institute for Network Science (YINS) and Yale's Department of Electrical Engineering, and I am honored to be supervised by Leandros Tassiulas. I use optimal control, dynamical systems, and game theory to study social, biological, and engineered networks. In particular, I am interested in understanding dynamic influence in human (and more generally, biological) networks.

As a postdoctoral associate at Cornell, I worked on networks of teams and their effect on the spread of opinions and influence, as well as various topics on the charging of electric vehicles. I obtained a PhD at Penn's Electrical and Systems Engineering Department under the tutelage of Saswati Sarkar and Santosh S. Venkatesh. My PhD thesis was on the optimal control of epidemics in the presence of heterogeneity. I looked at how a more granular view of the spread of an epidemic can help us to design better policies.
